Mock Interviews

Weekly Mock Interview Plan

Mocks are where preparation becomes performance. Run one mock per round-type per week in the final phase, always recorded, always debriefed against a rubric. This page is the schedule; the others are the scripts.

Plan

Overview

Do mocks out loud, recorded (audio is enough). The recording is the point — you'll hear the rambling, the missing tradeoffs, the unsupported claims. Debrief each one against the rubric and turn every weakness into a study task or a new golden-set-style note.

Interviewer script

For each mock: 45 min (5 clarify, 30 core, 10 follow-ups/debrief). Use a peer or self-interview from the question list. Record. Score with the rubric. Write 3 concrete fixes. Re-run the weakest round the next week.

Expected answer signals (strong)

  • You finish with energy and structure
  • You named tradeoffs unprompted
  • You said 'I don't know, here's how I'd find out' when stuck — cleanly
  • Your project stories had metrics

Weak answer signals

  • Ran out of time on the happy path
  • No eval/cost/safety mentioned
  • Folded on the second follow-up
  • Listed tools, not reasoning

Debrief checklist

  • Recorded it
  • Scored every rubric dimension
  • Wrote 3 concrete fixes
  • Logged any new concept gap
  • Scheduled a re-run of the weakest area
