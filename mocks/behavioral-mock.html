Mock · Senior Engineer Behavioral Round

A 45-minute behavioral round calibrated to 5–10 yr seniority: ownership, impact with metrics, ambiguity, conflict, and influence. Strong engineers lose offers here by under-selling scope or rambling.

Behavioral

Overview

Tests communication and seniority signal. Use STAR, lead with impact and metrics, and own decisions. Pair with the revision sheets.

Interviewer script

“Tell me about yourself.” (2 min, structured) → “A GenAI project you owned end to end.” → “A time you were wrong / it failed.” → “A disagreement with a stakeholder.” → “How you measured impact.” Probe for your specific decisions and the metric.

Questions

QTell me about a GenAI project you owned and its impact.SeniorbehavioralSeniorfreq: highBoth
Model answer

STAR: situation, your specific decisions, the architecture, the metric moved (latency/cost/quality/adoption).

Strong senior-level answer

Leads with business impact + a number; owns tradeoffs; credits the team without hiding own role.

Likely follow-ups
  • What was the hardest tradeoff?
  • What would you change?
Red-flag answer pattern
'We' with no 'I'; no metric.
QTell me about a time you were wrong or a project failed.SeniorbehavioralSeniorfreq: highBoth
Model answer

Honest failure, what you learned, what changed afterward.

Strong senior-level answer

Shows reflection + a systemic fix (e.g. added eval to prevent recurrence).

Likely follow-ups
  • What did you change systemically?
Red-flag answer pattern
A humblebrag or 'I work too hard'.

Expected answer signals (strong)

  • Structured, 2-min answers
  • Leads with impact + metric
  • Owns decisions ('I decided…')
  • Reflective on failure

Weak answer signals

  • Rambles, no structure
  • All 'we', no 'I'
  • No metrics
  • Defensive about failure

Debrief checklist

  • Did I use STAR?
  • Did every story have a metric?
  • Did I own decisions?
  • Was I concise?
